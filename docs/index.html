Oculo: A Multilabel Dataset for Identification of Ocular Abnormalities from Ultrasound Images

Ocular ultrasound (B-scan) is the preferred modality for assessing vitreoretinal health when media opacities obscure direct visualization, yet access to ophthalmologists skilled in its interpretation is limited in resource-constrained settings. Progress in automated analysis with deep learning (DL) has been constrained by the lack of public annotated datasets. We introduce Oculo, the first public dataset of ocular ultrasounds labeled for five vitreoretinal abnormalities, and systematically evaluate DL models on it to establish standardized baselines and outline avenues for future research in DL-based automated ocular ultrasound analysis.

Representative B-scan ultrasound images for the five evaluated ocular abnormalities.

Dataset

Oculo comprises 1,630 B-scan images from 1,242 patients, acquired over approximately four years on an Appasamy Marvel II ultrasound system (12 MHz) and exported as 900×900 PNGs. Two senior ophthalmologists independently annotated eight binary labels, with ambiguous cases adjudicated by a senior retina specialist. Five labels are evaluated; three rare conditions are released to support future research.

All released images are anonymized, with patient identifiers removed. Informed consent was obtained from all patients, and the study protocol was approved by the institutional review board. A fixed train/validation/test split (1,222 / 163 / 245) is provided with the dataset, available on Hugging Face.

Benchmark

We benchmark four image-classification backbones (EfficientNet-B0, ResNet50, VGG-19-BN, ViT-B-16) under single-task and multi-task settings, and three domain-specific foundation models (OpenUS, USFM, VisionFM) under full fine-tuning, linear probing, and linear-probe-then-finetune. EfficientNet-B0 achieves the best single-task performance (mean F1 = 0.92), while ViT-B-16 benefits most from multi-task training. Foundation-model linear probes reach performance comparable to the best task-specific model.
